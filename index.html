Amsterdam , Netherlands

Amsterdam is the capital city and most populous municipality of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, even though the Dutch government and the King reside in The Hague.

Late 12th century

The first records of the existence of Amsterdam, when fishermen and traders create a bridge with a dam across the river Amstel.

1275

Count Floris V grants the inhabitants of 'Amstelledamme' exemption from toll payments, effectively marking the city's founding date.

1300 - 1400s

Amsterdam receives city rights and joins the Hanseatic League, growing as a port and trading city.

1600s

Known as the Dutch Golden Age. Amsterdam becomes one of the wealthiest cities in the world. The Amsterdam Stock Exchange, the world's first stock exchange, is established.

1650s

Construction of the Canal Ring, which now is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

1800s

The Industrial Revolution sees Amsterdam expanding, but also suffering from problems like overcrowding and pollution.

1940 - 1945

World War II. Amsterdam, like the rest of the Netherlands, is occupied by Nazi Germany. The city's Jewish community, including Anne Frank, suffers greatly during the Holocaust.

Post-WWII

Amsterdam begins to modernize, becoming a cultural and social hub in Europe, and attracting immigration.

1970s - 1980s

The city becomes known for its liberal policies, including drug decriminalization

2000s - Present

Amsterdam is a bustling city, known for its history, culture, art (like the Van Gogh Museum and Rijksmuseum), and its modern economy..

"Amsterdam has more than 150 canals and 1,250 bridges, but it never seems crowded, nor bent and bitter from fleecing the tourist." (Julie Burchill)
